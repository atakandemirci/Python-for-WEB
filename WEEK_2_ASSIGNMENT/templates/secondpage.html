The Rise of Protectionism

This first post will probably not make much sense in the future. Nevertheless, I decided to write down what I've been babbling about. I hope that you'll keep supporting me and reading my posts every week. If you indulge me, I will do this every day…

I believe it is fair to name this period of time as "The Rise of Protectionism". There is a ongoing trade war among all the nations around the globe and it was started by President Trump. Imposing tariffs was a major pillar of his presidential campaign. As of today, even democratic presidential candidates are not mentioning if they intend to remove those tariffs. The reason may be the persistent trade deficit of the US economy.

The End of Unipolar Order

The United States of America (The US) has been the central player in the international system since the end of World War II. Also the US has been the leading actor in creation of new international organizations like the United Nations, NATO, IMF, and the World Bank. American diplomacy has been essential to multinational agreements on trade, regional security, arms control and climate. American diplomacy could and did claim to be at the center of international order.

We are in a process of transformation in the international order. This transformation of unipolar order to multipolar order is inevitable. But crucial point is the change of relations among the nations. The US had been gradually losing its dominant role in world affairs. A power shift among the nations has already begun by the end of the Cold War. The last 30 years have seen a redistribution of economic power among the world’s major powers on a scale that is probably never seen in history. And as of today some nations can assert independent and influential roles in regional economic and security developments.
